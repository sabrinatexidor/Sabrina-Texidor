1
⚡
Neurostimulation — VNS, RNS & DBS
Three FDA-Approved Implants — Not Traditional Brain Surgery
Three implanted devices approved when seizures don’t respond to medication. Yale and MGH perform all three:
  • VNS — chest implant, pulses to brain via vagus nerve. ~50% see meaningful reduction. Newer models auto-respond to incoming seizures.
  • RNS (NeuroPace) — “smart” implant reads brain in real time and stops seizures before they start. Up to 82% reduction at 6 years.
  • DBS — thalamus electrodes deliver continuous stimulation to reduce seizure activity.
Cost
Surgical — varies
Insurance
Medicare & most private insurance cover VNS & RNS
2
🥑
Ketogenic Diet Therapy
100 Years of Evidence — A Supervised Medical Therapy
High-fat, low-carb diet that changes how the brain uses fuel. Over 50% of people see 50%+ seizure reduction. Requires a neurologist and dietitian — not a DIY approach.
Where: Yale and Boston Children’s Hospital have dedicated adult keto epilepsy programs.
Cost
Dietitian visits vary
Insurance
Medically supervised nutrition often covered
3
🧬
Genetic Testing
Could Be the Missing Piece
Some epilepsy types respond only to one specific medication. Given Gio’s history, a genetic cause is worth ruling out. Testing can point directly to the right treatment.
Where: Yale and MGH both offer comprehensive epilepsy genetic testing — ask his neurologist.
Cost
Varies — ordered by neurologist
Insurance
Often covered when medically indicated
